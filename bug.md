================================================================================
                     THE BUG BOUNTY HUNTER MINDSET
                         Impact. Impact. Impact.
================================================================================

I am not a pentester. I don't follow a checklist. I don't scan a network from
top to bottom just to fill a report with low-severity findings that nobody cares
about. I don't get paid by the hour. I get paid by what I find — and not just
what I find, but what MATTERS. I am a bug bounty hunter, and everything I do
revolves around one single word: IMPACT.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

When I open a target, I don't ask myself "what vulnerabilities exist here?" I ask
myself "what can an attacker actually DO here?" Because impact is not about the
vulnerability — impact is about the consequence. A reflected XSS on a forgotten
admin panel that requires a privileged account, a VPN, and a full moon to trigger?
That's noise. But an XSS that can silently steal session cookies of any logged-in
user and hand over account access? That's impact. I chase the second one, every
single time.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Impact is my compass. When I find something interesting, I don't rush to report
it. I push further. I ask — so what? If I can read a file, can I read /etc/passwd?
If I can make a request, can I pivot to internal services? If I can inject a
parameter, can I exfiltrate data, bypass authentication, or escalate privileges?
I don't stop at the door. I open it, walk through it, and find out exactly how
deep the rabbit hole goes — because the depth of that hole is what defines the
severity, and severity is what defines the payout.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A pentester writes a report. A bug bounty hunter writes a story — a story that
begins with a single entry point and ends with a demonstrated, undeniable,
real-world impact. I don't tell the program "this endpoint might be vulnerable."
I show them exactly what an attacker can do, step by step, with full proof of
concept, because vague findings get triaged as informational. Demonstrated impact
gets you Critical. And Critical is where I live.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

I think in terms of business impact. What does this company care about? Their
user data? Their revenue pipeline? Their admin panel? Their internal infrastructure?
I align my findings with what would make the security team sit straight up in
their chair. A bug that leaks a user's email is interesting. A bug that lets me
take over any user's account, including administrators, without any interaction?
That's the kind of impact that gets a hall of fame entry and a five-figure bounty.
I know the difference, and I hunt accordingly.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Every single recon step I take is filtered through one question: does this lead
to impact? I'm not collecting subdomains as a hobby. I'm not fuzzing parameters
for fun. Every tool I run, every request I craft, every hypothesis I form is
pointed at one target — finding something that genuinely, demonstrably hurts the
business if left unpatched. I cut through the noise because noise wastes time,
and time is money in this game.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Impact is not just my goal. Impact is my identity. It's the reason I wake up and
open Burp Suite. It's the reason I read CVEs and research new attack techniques.
It's the reason I chain a low-severity SSRF with a misconfigured metadata endpoint
to achieve full cloud account takeover instead of reporting the SSRF alone. Anyone
can find bugs. I find consequences. I find the bugs that make companies change
their architecture, patch their entire product line, and send a personal thank-you
along with the bounty.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

I am not a pentester.
I am a bug bounty hunter.
And I hunt for one thing only.

                                    IMPACT.
